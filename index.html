Research

As a researcher, physical design area is in full of interesting and challenging problems, especially for the "next-generation" technologies. My research interests lie in technology-aware physical design methodology, design-technology co-optimization (DTCO), open-source EDA and machine learning-based prediction/optimization for physical design.

Technology-Aware Design Optimization

As technology advances, we see a lot of challenges (but interesting) in the digital VLSI physical design. For example, new device structures (GAA, Nanosheet, etc.), use of scaling boosters (Backside PDN, buried power rails, contact over active gate, etc.), large variations (in terms of leakage, timing, power, etc), complex and new design rules and large signoff margins (which means that new signoff methodology needed to reduce the margins). I have worked (and still work) on technology-aware (also known as manufacturing/process-aware) design optimization methodologies. My research interests lie in, but not limited to, technology-aware leakage/dynamic power reduction and performance improvement, IR-drop mitigation, routability evalutaion and enhancement at advanced technology nodes (sub-3nm).

Design-Technology Co-Optimization (DTCO)

Design-Technology Co-Optimization (DTCO) is beyond technology-aware design optimization. To be simple, technology should be well-defined with design considerations. Also, design should be optimized to be manufactured well and have best power, performance, area and cost (PPAC) of chips. At advanced nodes, DTCO is now getting more and more important both for foundry companies and chip designers to maximize their chip/technology competitiveness. There are so many technology and design options at an early stage of technology development. For example, there are too many combinations of standard-cell architectures to be evaluated along with technology definitions.

Open-Source EDA

Let's be honest. Academic research in the EDA area is not as hot as previously. I think the main reason is that everything is hidden under NDAs. All materials, PDKs, design rule manuals and IPs, of cutting edge technologies are hidden. All source codes from leading EDA companies are also hidden. To this end, academic researchers have an access to very limited (and old) ingredients for their research. In light of this "closed" community, the OpenROAD project has been launched in 2018. The goal of the project is to develop complete and fully open-sourced tools that achieve autonomous, 24-hour layout implementation (RTL to GDSII) with no humans in the loop. And yes, it is not as good as leading EDA tools for now, however, this project provides solid foundations for academic research, including shared databases, parsers for industry-standard formats, timing analysis, baseline codes for research, etc. I spent 2+ years on this project and I hope the project has leveraged academic physical design research.

Machine Learning in Physical Design

I am not a machine learning fanatic but machine learning is actually attractive for researchers. I am interested in several aspects in physical design to apply ML, especially where experienced human engineers are needed, e.g, floorplan, powerplan (PDN), macro placement, routing, timing and physical ECO, etc. Also, current commerical EDA tools are pretty good but still struggle with chicken-egg problems and heusitic approaches. This means that we still have the way to global optimum of physical design problems for the best PPAC. Machine learning can be a big helper and some works at industry already showed some perpectives and proofs over the recent years. I have a couple of works which applied ML to PDN and DTCO and also keep my eyes on interesting ML works in physical design.
